What we offer

Option 1: Workshops

Max. number of students: 30
Aimed at: Key Stage 3 pupils studying Spanish
Price: Half day (one group) - £350 - Full day (two groups) - £650

What happens in a workshop?

Students will:
  • Watch a live performance from professional Colombian musicians
  • Take part in an interactive music workshop of traditional Colombian rhythm and song
  • Learn about Colombian culture through images, videos, dialogue and lyrics
  • Reinforce curriculum vocabulary through instructions and lyrics
  • Compose and perform their own piece of music in Spanish, inspired by the workshop
  • Take part in a Q+A panel about life in Colombia

Option 2: Six- week programme

Max number of students: 30
Aimed at: Key Stage 3 students studying Spanish
Price: £3000 = £100 per head (this price stays the same even if less than 30 students take part)

What does the programme include?

  • 9 hours of 100% target language music and culture workshops over 6 weeks
  • Designed by qualified Spanish teachers and delivered by professional Colombian musicians (DBS)
  • Workshops around song, music, movement and composition all in Spanish
  • Performance alongside Latin American musicians
  • Access to studying languages at University

What makes us different

Contextualised learning

Native Speakers

Our workshops are led by musicians from Spanish speaking countries.

100% Target language

In order to recreate the immersion-experience of living abroad, all our workshops are delivered in 100% target language. All instructions and communication within the group will be in Spanish.

Global Awareness

Spending a sustained time with people from another culture will increase cultural sensitivity and global citizenship. Students will be encouraged to compare traditions and customs of the UK with Colombia, including new vocabulary, customs and traditions.

University and careers

One workshop will include a visit from language graduates who will share with students what it's like to learn a language at university and how it can benefit you in the future.

Engagement

A new way-in

Whilst some students may be turned off from learning a language in a classroom, using music is an an accessible medium for those hard to reach students, who simply need to experience language in a different context.Sessions are interactive and fast-paced and use a mixture of song, rhythm and movement to keep students engaged at all times.

Learn by doing – language in context

We are more likely to retain a language when it is used for a specific purpose. In our workshops the immediate focus is on learning music and students make progress in the language without realising! (77% of students in the pilot said that using music made it easier to learn Spanish.)

Attainment

Relevant curriculum

All workshops use structures and topics from the Key Stage 3* curriculum which reinforce familiar structures whilst introducing new vocabulary. Pupils will practice using all four skills of speaking and listening (communicating with facilitators and singing), reading (lyrics and texts) and writing (compositions). Work will directly feed into KS3 assessments and will be a perfect building block for the GCSE controlled assessment. *Key Stage 2 and 4 also available.

Academic Rigour

The familiar structures will be a base upon which to extend vocabulary and push students to experiment with the language exploring both Colombian culture and their own identity – all in the target language.

Regular and additional provision

At Pasaporte Cultural we believe that in order to counteract the low-provision in MFL and have a significant impact on attainment that workshops need to happen regularly and in addition to normal school time. This is why our six-week programme takes place after school and over a whole half term.

Personal Growth

Social SKills

Through workshops students will increase their team-work skills and will be encouraged to build positive relationships with other students on the programme.

Confidence

The opportunity to write and perform their own music on stage along side professional musicians in another language will have an undoubtable increase on pupils' confidence. This is a unique opportunity to really push them out of their comfort zone and test their resilience.
